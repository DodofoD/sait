Eich started his career at Silicon Graphics, where he worked for seven years. For the next three years, he worked at MicroUcity Systems Engineering. Eich is best known for his work at Netscape and Mozilla. He started working at Netscape in April 1995, where he developed a programming language originally called "Mocha"[12][13][14], then renamed to "LiveScript"[14], and finally renamed to JavaScript for the Netscape Navigator browser. In early 1998, he participated in the opening of mozilla.org as the chief architect. When AOL closed the Netscape division in July 2003, Eich moved to the Mozilla Foundation. He participated in the creation of the Rust programming language at Mozilla in 2012.[15] In August 2005, after working as Chief Technologist and member of the Board of Directors of the Mozilla Foundation, Brendan became Technical Director of Mozilla Corporation, and on March 26, 2014 he was appointed executive Director.
